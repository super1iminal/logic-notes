# Simplifying Statement Forms
This is not evaluating a statement form based on given truth values for statement variables, it is simplifying a statement form.
*see [[Evaluating Statement Forms]] to see how to evaluate a statement form*

One can simplify a statement form using a variety of laws. The following notes are where the laws are taken from:

[[Logical Equivalence]] (CONTAINS EVERYTHING)
- Commutative laws
- Associative laws
- Distributive laws
- Identity laws
- Negation laws
- Double negative law  (it is present in two notes)
- Idempotent laws
- Universal bound laws
- De Morgan's laws (it is present in two notes)
- Absorption Laws (it is present in two notes)
- Negations of $t$ and $c$ (it is present in two notes)

[[Complex Logic]]
- De Morgan's laws (with explanation)
- Double negative law (with explanation)

[[Tautologies and Contradictions]]
